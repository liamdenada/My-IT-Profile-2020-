Project Idea

My project idea started as a list of Countries that don’t allow you to view information on the internet that isn’t inherently evil. After looking further into the subject, I realized that most Countries have some level of security around the internet that they allow their citizens to access. Even here in Australia our information and messages are monitored for key words. As this is not a black and white issue, a moderate level of monitoring and censorship is needed to stop all malicious websites or websites with potentially illegal content. According to the world Tourism Organization the Asia Pacific Region had 348 million tourist arrivals just two years ago (International Tourism Highlights, 2019). This shows a giant potential market that might not have the information that they need to safely travel in the modern world. With applications with great user experience making up 85% of booking decisions made by travelers in 2019, all signs point to a web-based application (digital trends of the travel industry, 2020). With the world waiting for international travel to open back up it highlights a great market opportunity has arisen.
This project in its alpha will be able to tell anxious travelers exactly the level of censorship and overview that a Country's Government has over its internet. We are lucky enough to live in a Country that has a great respect for the freedoms of its citizens, only restricting a small amount of online content, most of which being harmful information or illegal content. Unfortunately, not all the world is like this. Many countries have used different laws and lack of public access to infrastructure to restrict the access of its citizens to much of the information freely provided on the internet. My project will be a web-based application to help plan travel and monitor the censorship and security of the internet in different areas. Not knowing what information, you can access in a particular country or better yet what google searches could land you in jail or worse is a much-needed security benefit that I know myself and many others would appreciate. The central project at alpha will require breaking the information into three separate branches of information. One central piece of information, The Countries, and two sub pieces of information, the magnitude of censorship and laws regarding internet and internet censorship. Along with a plan to use visual representation for the magnitude of internet censorship a country has, the project will also give a general rating. This general rating will be a cumulation of the level of censorship along with the laws of the country’s government. Some countries laws affect not only what websites you can look at but what you can say or do on any particular website or application. For example, North Korea has laws that restrict residents and visitors from accessing all websites and applications that they don’t deem align with their views. While researching this project I realized how hard it is to find information on internet censorship, especially a country governments' individual laws regarding it. Some countries information is very easy to find but others are non-existent. This motivated me further to create a central hub for this information and put it in the hands of anyone who needs it. The web-based application will use python to show the level of censorship of all countries and give information on select countries that you will need it for. The applications range will go from making sure you stay out of jail by not posting the wrong pictures to telling you which countries a VPN is suggested for. As this is a very linear information driven project, I was able to choose python to store and convey the information because it is a language, I'm relatively familiar with. It's in built functions and straight forward interface make it an easy choice for a first language.The basic skills required for this project are a very basic knowledge of the python programming language. After inputting the information into the list using my list function (my_list =[]) the information will be separated into 3 different parts the Country’s, The Laws and the Magnitude of Censorship. This information will then be converted to an easy to decipher display using HTML wrapping. The main issue with this information is that it's not easy to find in the walls of text, which Is why a simple clean visual display is very important. Using the linear search algorithm in Python and HTML wrapping the project will have a search function to help people get to their information destination faster (Output Data as an HTML File with Python, 2020). The ideal outcome of the project would be to create a product to educate the public the current reality of the state of censorship of the internet. Aswell as opening people's eyes to the pattern of consistent censorship of the internet and free information by many governments around the world.
